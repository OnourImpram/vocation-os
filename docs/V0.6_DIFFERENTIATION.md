# v0.6 Differentiation Contract

Status: implemented release contract. Comparative market leadership remains unclaimed until reproducible external baselines are executed.

VocationOS does not compete primarily on application volume. Version 0.6.0 turns its evidence, uncertainty, and authorization foundations into decision products that cannot be reduced to a generic job pipeline.

## Opportunity Truth Engine

The engine evaluates the trustworthiness and current actionability of an opportunity without labelling an employer or posting as fraudulent from weak signals.

Required outputs:

1. Posting liveness with observation time, source, and retrieval status.
2. Canonical company, role, location, and application endpoint identity.
3. Cross-source duplicate and contradiction records.
4. Salary, work authorization, licensing, relocation, and location claims with provenance and recency.
5. A bounded `unresolved`, `consistent`, `conflicting`, or `stale` classification for each material field.
6. Explicit separation between observed evidence and inferred risk indicators.

Acceptance gates:

1. No unsupported fraud or ghost-job conclusion.
2. Every material conclusion links to a timestamped source observation.
3. Expired or unreachable postings cannot enter an active campaign automatically.
4. Dedupe F1 is at least 0.97 and liveness precision is at least 0.98 on VocationBench.
5. Provider failure cannot silently convert missing evidence into a positive match.

Reference ontologies: [ESCO](https://esco.ec.europa.eu/en/use-esco) and [O*NET Web Services](https://services.onetcenter.org/about).

## Career Assurance Case

Each consequential recommendation can produce a machine-readable assurance case rather than only a score or narrative.

The case will bind:

1. Decision and route identifiers.
2. Supporting claims and source evidence.
3. Material uncertainty and recency limits.
4. Known defeaters and unresolved specialist questions.
5. Reversibility and high-stakes policy decisions.
6. Human approval scope and expiry.
7. Action receipts and observed outcomes.
8. Model, policy, taxonomy, and data versions.

Acceptance gates:

1. An unresolved hard defeater prevents a positive action recommendation.
2. The assurance case is reproducible from the encrypted event history.
3. A changed claim, policy, or source invalidates the previous case deterministically.
4. The generator cannot certify its own assurance case.
5. Export redacts private claims unless their allowed-use policy explicitly permits disclosure.

## Credential Passport

The passport imports and exports standards-based achievement evidence while preserving issuer, subject, criteria, validity, and signature status.

Acceptance gates:

1. Support Open Badges 3.0 compatible achievement credentials with real Compact JWS and `eddsa-rdfc-2022` verification, without treating self-assertion as issuer verification.
2. Preserve the original credential and verification result in the encrypted artifact vault.
3. Convert credentials into career claims only through explicit mapping and allowed-use review.
4. Expired, revoked, unverifiable, or audience-restricted credentials cannot become public claims automatically.
5. Exported evidence packages remain independently verifiable.

The default daemon resolver is offline. It uses pinned standard contexts and local `did:key` resolution. HTTPS issuer documents require an explicitly supplied bounded loader. Formal schema, revocation, and refresh checks remain separate from signature verification and may leave a passport entry incomplete.

Reference standard: [Open Badges 3.0](https://www.imsglobal.org/spec/ob/v3p0/).

## Positioning Gate

Career Ops already demonstrates broad portal scanning, ATS document generation, interview preparation, negotiation support, pipeline integrity, and a TUI. VocationOS should claim superiority only when VocationBench demonstrates that the assurance, truth, and credential layers improve decision quality or safety against a reproducible baseline.
